      Position: Chief Financial Officer

      Eligibility: Applicants and staff must be authorized to work for any employer in the United States. PLEZi Nutrition will not sponsor an employment visa for this position.

      Location: Remote in Eastern or Central time zones.

      Reports to: Chief Executive Officer

      Salary Range: Provided by People and Culture


      About PLEZi Nutrition


      PLEZi Nutrition is a new kids’ brand, created to bring higher standards on how we make and market food and beverages for our kids. PLEZi represents the voices of parents everywhere looking for more practical, healthier options. PLEZi’s first product is a kids’ drink with no added sugar and 75% less sugar than average leading 100% fruit juices. The U.S. is in a nutrition-related health crisis and sugary drinks are the leading source of added sugar: nearly two-thirds of youth consume sugary drinks each day. PLEZi also contains fiber and nutrients to support kids’ growing bodies. PLEZi the product, the education platform, and the philanthropic efforts are here to help raise a healthier generation of kids and have some fun along the way.



      About JUGGERNAUT CAPITAL


      Juggernaut Capital Partners (JCP) is a leading private equity firm focusing on lower middle market companies, primarily in the consumer and healthcare sectors. Juggernaut’s portfolio includes brands and companies like PLEZi, ZOA Energy, Advantage Solutions, French Transit, Voss Water, True Citrus, and many others. Juggernaut has a family of funds representing well over $1 billion of capital commitments and is currently investing out of JCP IV, L.P.



      What You'll Do:


      As a key member of the executive management team, the Chief Financial Officer for PLEZi Nutrition will report to the CEO and assume a strategic role in the management of the company. Most importantly, they will ensure the financial health of the business through effective planning and analysis, and cash management. The CFO will focus on building long-term shareholder value by managing the path of growth and driving profitability through efficient operations.

      The CFO will ensure that effective strategies, policies, and procedures are in place to achieve profitability as the PLEZi business continues to build momentum across the U.S. As a seasoned business leader, the CFO will build effective infrastructure within the organization and successfully negotiate with key external business partners. They will advise on allocation of capital for business operations and asset procurement and ensure the accurate reporting of financial activities and maintain compliance with respect to taxes, insurance and other regulatory requirements. They will establish and maintain adequate risk management strategies to ensure liquidity and mitigate liability for PLEZi Nutrition.

      SCOPE OF RESPONSIBILITIES


      • Vision & Mission – The CFO will need to support and enhance the current vision for PLEZi Nutrition, long-term. PLEZi is transforming how consumers and retailers think about healthy snacks and beverages for children, by establishing new trends and getting out-front with Innovation. The vision today is bold, and we need a CFO who can manage all financial aspects and add value with operational insights to make this bold vision a reality.


      • Strategic Plan – Support and guide management on all aspects of PLEZi’s strategic, annual operating and capital plans. Help shepherd the business through a high-growth phase with a focus on profitability and maintenance of adequate working capital to support operations and key initiatives focused on optimizing all operational areas. The CFO will work closely with the CEO, COO, Sales, the Chief People & Culture Officer, the Chief Marketing Officer, the Chief Supply Chain Officer, and others to accurately incorporate initiatives and assumptions into the respective plans.


      • Shareholder/Investor Value – Maintain a constant focus on opportunities to increase shareholder value by focusing on all opportunities to maximize the long-term financial health of PLEZi. Lead ongoing analysis of sales and margin by product, including establishing optimal inventory levels, to reduce cash burn and minimize debt. Work with Supply Chain to gain efficiency in Purchasing, Production, Warehousing and Shipping. Thoroughly analyze spending in key categories such as Marketing to evaluate ROI. Review variances to plan for all general and administrative expenses and address accordingly. Advise the CEO and Board on all options related to potential exit events, down the road.


      • Financial Reporting & Forecasting – Produce accurate, timely and actionable financial and operational reports for peers, for the CEO and COO, and for the PLEZi Board. Prepare quarterly reports and presentations for the Board, which provide insights into the results of operations, cash flows and working capital. Communicate and evaluate variances to plans, prepare, refine and revise forecasts to reflect updated assumptions from operational departments. Lead optimization of the ERP system, expand the existing forecasting process into an integrated business process with collaborative input from the Sales, Marketing, R&D and Supply Chain teams. Develop additional statistical models and meaningful metrics to evaluate and improve accuracy across all functions.

      • Capital Expenditures – Ensure that all capital expenditures for PLEZi have been reviewed and approved in connection with the capital plan. Create a process to prioritize CAPEX based on business needs and ROI. Where necessary establish policies, procedures and spending limits by functional area to eliminate unauthorized spending. Perform lease vs. buy/build analysis for major equipment spending and make recommendations to CEO and COO.

      • Profitability – The CFO will be the driving force in delivering profit improvements. They will perform ongoing profitability analyses at the product, distributor partner, customer and vendor levels, to identify potential opportunities for improvement. They will work closely with the Chief Supply Chain Officer to support cost effective and efficient operations activities including demand planning, production, supply chain logistics, contract manufacturing, customer service and third-party vendors and suppliers. The CFO will ensure key controls, policies and procedures are in-place across the PLEZi organization to guide business decisions, monitor expenditures and identify improvements to gross margins and profitability.

      • Optimize Operations – Along with the CEO, COO, Chief Supply Chain Officer, and Sales, develop and execute plans to continually optimize capacity and capability of the Company’s operations to meet current and future product demand, including, but not limited to, implementation of ERP systems, processing, co-packing, warehousing and shipping. Monitor SKU levels and recommend SKU rationalization to consistently optimize inventory levels. Collaborate with the COO on the development and protection of IP to increase PLEZi’s enterprise value.


      • Treasury and Cash Management – Optimize cash management and conduct regular analysis of cash flow. Maintain 13-week cash flow models to a high degree of accuracy. Oversee raw materials availability/procurement; hands on leadership of strategic cash outflows, ensuring appropriate levels of production and inventory to fulfill demand. Develop and manage tax strategies.


      • Risk Management/Compliance - Perform risk assessment across the PLEZi organization and evaluate exposure. Maintain or develop adequate internal control safeguards. Evaluate and manage insurance coverage. Manage relationships with banks and auditors. Oversee annual audit of financial reports and ensure record systems compliance with U.S. GAAP. Work with supply chain and sales to verify that operations teams/facilities and third-party vendors and service providers comply with state and federal health, safety, and environmental policies and procedures. Maintain record management systems compliance across the organization.



      Minimum Requirements

      • Bachelor's degree in finance, Business, or related field, and be a CPA or CMA. Master’s Degree is preferred.
      • At least 15 years of progressive Accounting, Finance, and Operations experience, with 5 years in executive finance leadership roles in CPG. Experience should cover the Scope of Responsibilities outlined above and align with the Skills and Personality traits outlined below.
      • Foundational experience in CPG, with a large blue-chip Beverage (or Food) company,
      • Recent experience with a VC-backed or PE-owned Beverage (or Food) start-up, or early-stage business, focusing on rapid market share growth.
      • Proven experience in taking a company through a rapid growth phase while judiciously managing cash and debt.
      • Demonstrated ability to migrate and enhance Accounting, Reporting, and implementing ERP systems to a higher degree of sophistication.
      • Demonstrated analytical and strategic thinking skills, with the ability to translate complex data into actionable insights and timely decisions.
      • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills, with the ability to build relationships and influence stakeholders at all levels of the organization.
      • Demonstrated ability to lead change and drive a culture of innovation across the organization.
      • Proven track record of proactively building internal and external partnerships.
      • Demonstrated commitment to a high degree of internal and external service delivery.
      • Proven commitment to diversity, inclusion, and fostering an environment of belonging.
